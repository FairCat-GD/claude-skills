# Research playbook

A shallow search returns the same 10 places every guidebook lists. Your job is to go deeper — that's where the lifelong memories come from.

## Source priority

Trust these, in order:

1. **Official sites** — museums, attractions, city tourism boards. Authoritative for hours, prices, booking rules.
2. **Established travel publications** — The Guardian's city guides, Condé Nast Traveler, Lonely Planet, NYT 36 Hours series, Time Out local editions, Atlas Obscura (great for hidden gems).
3. **Local-language sources** — local newspapers' "best of" lists, neighborhood blogs. Translate if needed; locals know.
4. **Specialty communities** — r/AskCulinary or local food forums for restaurants, r/photography for shooting spots, dedicated history forums for niche sites.
5. **Reddit threads** for the destination — especially `/r/[city]` posts asking "what's underrated here". Filter for engagement and recency.

Avoid AI-generated content farms (often have generic descriptions, sometimes wrong hours/locations). If a page reads like a template with five attractions per paragraph, skip it.

## Search plan

Run these searches before drafting. Run them in parallel where you can. Treat each as a probe — read multiple results, don't stop at the first hit.

### Core
- `"[city] top attractions"` — catalog the unmissables
- `"[city] hidden gems"` / `"[city] off the beaten path"` — catalog non-obvious finds
- `"things to do in [city] locals love"` — locals' picks tend to be different
- `"[city] best neighborhoods to walk"` — for connective tissue between sights
- `"[city] day trip"` — only if user has car or strong rail link

### Per interest (only those the user selected)
- `"[city] best art museums"` / `"contemporary art [city]"` / `"street art [city]"`
- `"[city] historical sites must see"` / `"[city] history walking tour"` / `"[city] cultural traditions"` / `"[city] local customs"` — the interest is **History & culture**, so research both the historical sites *and* living culture (traditions, cultural quarters, local institutions), not history alone
- `"[city] architecture highlights"` / `"[architect] buildings in [city]"` for famous-architect cities
- `"[city] parks gardens"` / `"hiking near [city]"`
- `"[city] nightlife"` / `"best bars [city]"`
- `"[city] food experience"` / `"[city] food markets"` / `"cooking class [city]"`
- `"[city] with kids"` / `"family friendly [city]"`
- `"photogenic spots [city]"` / `"viewpoints [city]"`

### Food (if opted in)
- For each budget tier requested:
  - `"best cheap eats [city]"` / `"[city] street food"` (cheaper)
  - `"best restaurants [city] mid-range"` / `"[city] neighborhood bistro"` (mid)
  - `"[city] fine dining"` / `"Michelin [city]"` (higher-end)
- `"[city] regional specialties"` — what to actually order
- Check restaurants' own sites for current hours, closed days, reservation policy

### Shopping (if opted in)
- `"[city] shopping district"` for each user-named category
- `"[city] souvenirs what to buy"` for souvenirs specifically
- `"[city] markets"` for local goods

### Safety
- `"[city] tourist scams"` — specific named scams, not generic warnings
- `"[city] pickpocket areas"` / `"safe neighborhoods [city]"`
- `"[city] safety tips"` — official tourism board advisory
- `"emergency number [country]"` — police, ambulance, fire
- Note dress codes, cultural rules (shoes off, hair covered, etc.) for any religious sites in plan

### City facts, curiosities & local tastes (Appendix F)
- `"[city] famous for what"` / `"[city] facts surprising"` / `"[city] quirks history"`
- `"[city] local food drinks must try"` / `"[city] regional specialty alcohol"` / `"what locals drink in [city]"`
- Search for the **stories** as much as the facts — origin myths (Vienna's first coffee house ⇆ Lviv's Kulczycki), bizarre festivals, the dish you didn't know was invented here. These are what give Appendix F its warmth.

### Snack / drink stops (optional, when on-route)
For each day's route, briefly think: is there a famous, characteristic, *small* bite or drink that happens to be passed by? Search:
- `"[neighborhood] famous coffee bar"` / `"[city] iconic street snack"`
- `"[city] famous café locals"` / `"[city] best ice cream gelato"` (etc.)

Only add a snack stop if the answer is yes AND it's ≤2 min off the route. The bar in Florence for *bombolone*, the macarons window on the Champs-Élysées, the Pyana Vyshnya kiosk on Lviv's Soborna — these are the targets. Generic cafes don't qualify.

### Weather (only if dates or at least a season are known — otherwise skip)
- Exact dates within ~14 days: fetch the actual day-by-day forecast.
- Exact dates 15+ days out, or only a rough month/season: `"[city] weather in [month]"` for typical temperatures, rainfall, daylight hours — present as averages, not a forecast.
- No dates and no season: skip weather research, and omit the appendix.
- Either way, note any seasonal closures (some sites close in winter, some restaurants close in August).

## What to look up per attraction

For every place that survives the prioritization cut, find:

- **Current opening hours** (note if they vary by day or season)
- **Entry fee** (adult, plus any concessions worth mentioning)
- **Booking requirement** (advance timed ticket? skip-the-line worth it?)
- **Typical visit duration** (so the day plan is realistic)
- **Best time to visit** (morning quiet, golden hour, etc.)
- **Closed days** (Mondays for many European museums)
- **Specific tips** that don't appear on the first page of Google

If you can't confirm hours/fees from an authoritative source, write `(verify on site)` — never invent.

## Prioritization

After research, you'll have far more candidates than fit. Cut by:

1. **Match to interests** — if user said "Architecture", a Gaudí site beats a so-so museum.
2. **Match to focus** — Famous vs. hidden vs. mix. Honor what they asked for.
3. **Geographic clustering** — a great spot far from everything else is expensive in time; sometimes drop it.
4. **Visit cost** — full-day commitments (palaces, theme parks) crowd out other things; only include if signal-of-interest is strong.
5. **Closure timing** — if it's closed every day of the trip, it's out, no matter how good.

## When research stalls

If a destination is obscure and search returns little:
- Try the local-language name in search
- Search for the country + region instead of just city
- Use travel blogs and Reddit threads as starting points to find names of places, then verify each name on official sources
- Be transparent with the user: "Coverage on [place] is thin online. I've focused on the best-documented spots."
