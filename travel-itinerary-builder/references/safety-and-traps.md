# Safety & traps appendix — checklist

The "Things to watch out for" appendix is one of the highest-value parts of the itinerary. Generic warnings ("be careful") are useless. Cite specific, named scams and concrete risks for the destination.

## What to include

### Common local scams (name them)
Every popular destination has known scams. Examples to give you a sense of the specificity required:
- Paris: ring scam, petition signers near Sacré-Cœur, fake police asking for wallet inspection
- Rome: rose-givers, friendship bracelets at the Spanish Steps, restaurant menu-switching near major sights
- Barcelona: La Rambla pickpockets, fake football jerseys
- Bali: rigged taxi meters from the airport, fake police on scooters
- NYC: Times Square costumed characters demanding tips, three-card monte

Look these up specifically for the destination. List the 3–6 most common, with one-line "how it works / how to avoid".

### Pickpocket hotspots
Be specific: which metro lines, which squares, which streets. Crowded tourist areas, public transport during rush hour, and sit-down restaurants where bags hang on chairs are the usual culprits.

### Neighborhoods or streets to avoid
Especially at night. Be factual, not alarmist. Cite the source if it's a known advisory.

### Tourist traps
Restaurants with menus in 8 languages near major sights, overpriced "traditional" performances, "free" tours that pressure for tips. Name the specific traps in this city when known.

### Cultural / legal rules a foreigner could miss
- Dress codes for religious sites (covered shoulders, knees, hair)
- Photography restrictions (no photos inside, no flash, drone laws)
- Public drinking laws (illegal in some places)
- Tipping norms (mandatory? insulting?)
- Public displays of affection where they cause issues
- Drug laws that differ from home

### Health & water
- Tap water safety
- Specific food risks if relevant
- Pharmacy norms (some require prescriptions for things that are OTC at home)

## Emergency contacts section

Must include:

- **Single emergency number** if the country has one (911 US, 112 EU, 999 UK, 110/119 Japan, etc.)
- **Police direct line** if different
- **Ambulance** if different
- **Fire** if different
- **Tourist police hotline** if one exists (Spain, Italy, Greece, Thailand, others have these)
- **Country code** for dialing in
- **Roaming dialing notes** — does the user need to dial `+[country code]` from a foreign SIM? Is there a leading `0` to drop?

If findable:
- **Nearest 24h pharmacy** to the accommodation
- **Nearest hospital with English-speaking staff** if user is traveling somewhere with a language barrier
- **User's home country embassy address and phone** in the destination country

## Format

Keep each item tight — one or two lines. The user is scanning this on their phone in a tricky moment, not reading prose.

Example:
```
**Three-shell game (Las Ramblas):** Cardboard table, fast hands. You will lose. Walk past.
**Metro line 5 between Diagonal and Sagrada Familia:** Most active pickpocket route. Keep bag in front.
**Restaurants with photo menus on La Rambla:** Avoid. Walk two streets inland for the same food at half the price.
```
